title: "Ditonic Data Hub Platform Enhancement"
role: "Data Engineer"
year: 2024
outcomeSummary: "Improved data platform ingestion performance: measured the bottlenecks of a row-by-row single-statement JDBC ingest path with per-stage observability (Jaeger/Prometheus/Grafana, then Datadog), corrected the ingest method and Kafka broker topology, and made even the Spark black-box stage visible end-to-end"
overview: "Owned the ingestion-pipeline performance analysis and improvement for Ditonic, a Hadoop-ecosystem data hub platform. Data arrives on two tracks — real-time and batch — and instead of guessing why ingest throughput was low, I pinned the cause with per-stage measurement and improved the producer (middle) → queue (Kafka) → storage (Hive/HBase) path from an end-to-end perspective."
problem: "Both real-time and batch data were ingested into the DB row by row, sending a single query statement at a time through a JDBC interface. Hive, the primary ingest target, is suited to large batch loads, yet queries were processed one by one, so actual ingest performance was low. On top of that, Kafka was configured with a single broker rather than the minimum of three needed for replication and fault tolerance, making the pub/sub stage a bottleneck; and HBase, whose syntax differs from standard SQL, sat behind Apache Phoenix translating ANSI queries — workable but not fully smooth. Above all, the internal Spark stage was a black box, making it hard to pinpoint the bottleneck precisely."
constraints:
  - "Data arrives on two tracks simultaneously — real-time and batch"
  - "The primary store, Hive, favors large batch loads — a poor fit for row-by-row processing"
  - "HBase differs from standard SQL and must be accessed through a Phoenix translation layer"
  - "Spark internals are a black box — detailed performance metrics cannot be measured from outside"
  - "The Dataset definition layer has dependencies — a parent definition must take precedence over its children"
approach: "First, I measured the time spent in each data-ingest stage with Jaeger, Prometheus, and Grafana to locate the bottleneck. From the view that a fast DB transaction alone is useless unless the producer (middle) and the queue (Kafka) keep up, I switched row-by-row single-statement ingest to bulk/batch ingest, and moved Kafka from a single broker to a multi-broker (3) topology that provides replication and fault tolerance, clearing the pub/sub bottleneck. HBase access kept the Phoenix ANSI→HBase translation layer. For black-box stages like Spark internals that are hard to instrument yourself, I introduced Datadog (SaaS) to cover the observability gap."
keyDecisions:
  - decision: "Switch row-by-row single-statement ingest to bulk/batch ingest"
    reasoning: "Both real-time and batch data were being ingested row by row as single query statements over JDBC, and the primary target, Hive, is a store suited to large batch loads — a poor fit for that method. Changing the ingest unit to bulk/batch secured throughput aligned with the store's characteristics."
    alternatives:
      - "Keep single-statement + tune the DB"
      - "Replace the store itself"
  - decision: "Move Kafka from a single broker to a multi-broker (3) topology for replication and fault tolerance"
    reasoning: "With a single broker, the pub/sub stage was a bottleneck where data could not flow smoothly. Moving to a multi-broker (minimum 3) topology with replication and fault tolerance raised the throughput and stability of the producer→queue→storage flow."
    alternatives:
      - "Keep the single broker + tune partitions/consumers"
      - "Replace with a different message queue"
  - decision: "Introduce Datadog (SaaS) to observe black-box stages"
    reasoning: "Jaeger/Prometheus/Grafana made per-stage measurement possible, but Apache Spark internals were a black box whose detailed performance metrics could not be measured from outside. Rather than hand-rolling all the instrumentation, I adopted the ready-made observability service Datadog to gain end-to-end visibility through the Spark stage."
    alternatives:
      - "Build Spark instrumentation in-house"
      - "Only extend the Jaeger/Prometheus/Grafana stack"

  - "Matching the ingest method to the store's nature (Hive = large batch loads) can be a more fundamental improvement than tuning"
